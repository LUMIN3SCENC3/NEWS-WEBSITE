Amihan resurgence brings cool weather in parts of Philippines

Weather | February 18, 2026 10:58am | by ARIEL ROJAS

A strong surge of the northeast monsoon or Amihan is affecting Luzon and the Visayas this Wednesday, February 18, 2026.

Despite this surge, temperature drops in Benguet have not been significant: Baguio recorded 15.0°C while La Trinidad posted 15.1°C.

Aside from cool weather, Amihan is also bringing light to occasionally moderate rains over the eastern Cordillera, Cagayan Valley, Aurora, Quezon, Oriental Mindoro, Bicol Region, and parts of Eastern Visayas. Amihan is expected to prevail over Luzon through the weekend.

Meanwhile, a shear line re-formed due to the convergence of the Amihan and the Easterlies.

Moderate to heavy rains are being experienced in Caraga and Davao Region, while scattered rains may also affect other parts of Mindanao. From Friday, the shear line is forecast to retreat northward to Visayas, where it will also bring significant rainfall through the weekend.

Flooding and landslides are possible in these areas, especially during periods of heavy and prolonged rainfall. The Amihan season usually peaks in January and February and ends by the third or fourth week of March.
