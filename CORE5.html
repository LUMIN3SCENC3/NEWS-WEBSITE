PCO chief denies alleged ‘harassment’ of presidential son's convoy

Government | February 22, 2026 12:43am | by HARLENE DELGADO

Presidential Communications Office Secretary Dave Gomez denied the alleged ‘harassment’ of an unnamed presidential son’s convoy along Northern Luzon Expressway (NLEX) Saturday night.

"No such incident involving any presidential son,” Gomez said in message to Palace reporters.

A social media post claimed that a “three-vehicle motorcade blocked the presidential son’s vehicle and an estimated 15 men alighted from the vehicles reported to be registered to a municipality in Bulacan.”

“The Bulacan registered vehicles allegedly occupied the three NLEX lanes when the vehicle carrying the presidential son overtook the Bulacan vehicles, which apparently was the reason of the supposed harassment,” it added.
