AMA Education System backs medical mission in Negros Occidental

Education | February 9, 2026 12:00am | by PHILSTAR

Even as Bago City grappled with the aftermath of Typhoon Verbena, the AMA Education System (AMAES) pushed through with its commitment to community healthcare, joining the second leg of the nationwide “Lab for All” program in Negros Occidental.

Held in Bago City, the medical mission served more than 1,500 residents, many from vulnerable and underserved sectors, who sought consultations, basic diagnostic services and medical guidance amid limited access to healthcare.

The effort was led by the AMA School of Medicine, with support from medical volunteers and students of AMA Computer College Bacolod.

Physicians and students provided free consultations and preventive care services, underscoring the importance of early detection at a time when health services were strained by weather-related disruptions.

The activity formed part of Lab for All, a nationwide healthcare initiative spearheaded by First Lady Louise Liza Araneta-Marcos to bring essential medical services closer to communities.

In Bago, the mission was carried out in coordination with the Department of Health and other government agencies to ensure alignment with national health priorities.

Beyond service delivery, the mission reflected AMAES’ emphasis on integrating education with community service.

Medical students took on active roles, gaining firsthand experience in community-based healthcare, inter-agency coordination and responding to health needs in crisis settings.

During the activity, the First Lady expressed her appreciation for the continued support of Amable Aguiluz V and Amable Aguiluz IX, citing their sustained involvement in advancing the goals of Lab for All and highlighting the role of private educational institutions in national healthcare efforts.

As the mission concluded, organizers said it left more than completed consultations and filled prescriptions, reinforcing the idea that education paired with service can be a powerful tool for nation-building

In Bago City, amid recovery efforts and uncertainty, the presence of AMAES and its volunteers served as a reminder that accessible, compassionate healthcare remains a cornerstone of community resilience
