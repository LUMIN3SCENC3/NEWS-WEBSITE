‘It’s me, Marina’: Drag star Marina Summers comes out as trans woman

Lifestyle | February 7, 2026 11:44pm | by MARILAG ODTOHAN

Filipino drag artist Marina Summers has come out as trans woman, revealing that she began her transition in 2025 and describing it as a beautiful step in embracing her true self.

The announcement came in an Instagram post on Saturday, February 7, where she shared her deep, personal milestone with her fans, friends, and followers.

For Marina, the confession has been a long time coming, admitting that she had been sitting with her truth for a while, but as she puts it, "today feels like that moment."

Reflecting on her past, Marina confessed that, from a young age, she had always felt a deep connection to her feminine side, even in moments when she didn’t fully understand how to express it.

"Becoming the person I've always seen myself as felt like something I'd get to "later," something I gently pushed to the side while I tried to catch every other dream first," she said. This internal realization remained with her, quietly shaping her identity as she grew.

It wasn’t until last year that Marina gave herself permission to stop pausing and to finally begin her transition.

In her post, she made it clear that the decision to open up about her transition was not one made hastily. She had taken the time to reflect and wait for the right moment, choosing to navigate her journey privately—free from the pressures of public opinion.

"I just really needed space to feel it, to heal, to simply exist in my own skin without the outside noise. I'm grateful I gave myself that," Marina shared.

Now, Marina feels ready to share more of her journey, opening the door a little wider for others to walk with her. She also acknowledged that the person known to many as “Yan” or “Adrian” was an important part of her life, but that chapter is now happily closed.

“I’d love for you to get to know me as the woman I’m becoming,” she said. Marina admitted that there is still much to learn, but she knew she wanted to be more honest with herself and the people around her.
Marina has long been a force to be reckoned with in the world of drag and entertainment.
In 2022, she gained widespread recognition as a standout competitor on "Drag Race Philippines" season 1, where she finished as a runner-up.
Her talent, charisma, and unique perspective quickly earned her a dedicated fanbase and the respect of the drag community. But that was only the beginning.

In 2024, Marina made history by becoming the first DRPH alum to compete on "RuPaul’s Drag Race: UK vs The World," a prestigious international franchise that allowed her to showcase her artistry to a global audience. It was a groundbreaking moment not only for Marina but for the Filipino drag scene as a whole.
The following year, Marina made history once again by becoming the first Filipino drag artist to walk the red carpet at the Cannes Film Festival.
Marina also released her own music, headlined "RuPaul's Drag Race Live" in Las Vegas, and starred in the opening of Miss Universe Philippines 2024, further cementing her status as a groundbreaking performer and a true cultural icon.

As Marina continues to embrace this new chapter in her life, there is no doubt that her journey will inspire countless others.
Her story is not just her own. It is a story that resonates with anyone who has ever struggled to accept themselves, who has faced the fear of living in their truth, or who has sought the courage to become who they truly are.
In this new chapter of her life, Marina is reminding everyone that the path to self-acceptance is never easy—but embracing who you are is the ultimate act of defiance, strength, and empowerment.
