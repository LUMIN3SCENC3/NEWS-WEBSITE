UAAP: Eshana Nunag’s steady play key to La Salle’s early success

Volleyball | February 22, 2026 8:50pm | by LANCE AGCAOILI

Eshana Nunag continues to flourish under the pressure-packed atmosphere of the UAAP women’s volleyball tournament that comes with orchestrating La Salle’s offense.

Before 15,350 fans, Nunag displayed another steady playmaking for La Salle with 16 excellent sets and three points in a dominant sweep of University of Santo Tomas to stay unbeaten in Season 88 on Sunday at Mall of Asia Arena.

“Compared to my [first game] with FEU, I was able to see what the UAAP level is really like, so I had a better idea of what to expect coming into this game. After our last game, my goal is just to keep improving and keep learning,” said Nunag in Filipino after DLSU’s 25-14, 25-15, 26-24 victory.

The rookie setter has shown maturity beyond years, proving to be a perfect fit to main offensive weapons Shevana Laput, Angel Canino, and Amie Provido.

“I think it’s really about mindset, treating it like training, focusing on my teammates and the coaches, and not paying too much attention to the crowd,” said Nunag. “I just think about the people who matter most, the guidance of the coaches and the support of my teammates inside the court.”

La Salle deputy coach Noel Orcullo believes that the La Salle Integrated School product could be the missing piece for their team.

“For now, we’re seeing her steadiness. From the opening game, she has shown she can keep up with veteran setters. I hope she continues that progress and hopefully she becomes the stable setter we’ve been looking for in this tournament,” said Orcullo in Filipino after his squad improved to 2-0.

“Eshana isn’t new to us. Since senior high school, she’s already been training with the team. We incorporated her early into Coach Ramil’s system. What she really needed was exposure — getting used to the crowd and this level of competition. The adjustment has been there since senior high; she’s already adapted.”
